Alvaro Diaz has had several albums throughout his career, such as 'HATO REY', which was his first album, and some more such as 'DIAZ ANTES', 'SAN JUAN GRAND PRIX', 'FELICILANDIA' and his latest album 'SAYONARA', but it should be noted that the artist only recognizes the last two as official albums, the first ones for him are studio albums, meaning they are not official, below I will explain the most relevant and famous albums that Alvaro Diaz has released.

DIAZ ANTES (2020)

This is a conceptual release divided into five radio stations, each appearing occasionally as skits, featuring vocal collaborations by C. Tangana, Cazzu, Yandel, Fuego, Lyanno, Kyoshi Reyes, Miky Woodz, and Papi Sousa. Many of the tracks have been previously released, and up to six previously unreleased tracks can be heard on a 17-track tracklist.

FELICILANDIA

Álvaro Díaz's album "Felicilandia" is a nostalgic exploration through 16 songs, where the artist searches for happiness and finds it in family and in seeing things from afar. The album's concept also includes the use of Puerto Rican slang, as reflected in songs like "Problemón." The album was developed during the pandemic, and the artist conceives it as a search for happiness, like the movie "Pursuit of Happiness."

SAYONARA

Álvaro Díaz's album "Sayonara" is a conceptual narrative that explores the experience of saying goodbye to a relationship. It begins with the breakup and continues through various emotional stages, from trying to forget your ex at the club to accepting the farewell. The album includes a variety of musical genres, including rap, electronic music, and reggaeton, and features guest appearances by artists such as Rauw Alejandro, Quevedo, and paopao.And the Puerto Rican singer had a big impact; he was on the cover of Rolling Stones for the first time.

ABOUT ALVARO DÍAZ

Jorge Álvaro Díaz Rodríguez (San Juan, July 25, 1995) is a Puerto Rican singer and songwriter. Throughout his musical career, he has collaborated with several artists such as Tainy, Bad Bunny, Sebastián Yatra, Rawayana, Feid, Zion, Eladio Carrión, Yandel, Dalex, Cazzu, Rauw Alejandro, Mora, Jowell & Randy, Myke Towers, Jhayco, Nathy Peluso, Quevedo, Fuego, C. Tangana, among others. Álvaro Díaz was born on July 25, 1995, in San Juan, Puerto Rico. He is the son of Jorge Díaz Reverón, a senior judge of the First Court of the Puerto Rican municipality of Caguas. In addition to his music, he has also worked as a publicist, but after improvising on tracks and recording, his melodies gained popularity in the Latin market. Álvaro Díaz began his musical career after uploading songs to SoundCloud in 2012, highlighting his debut track, "Chica de la Isla." During this time, he gained significant recognition, which led him to continue releasing songs. After his debut on the music scene, he continued releasing singles steadily between 2015 and 2016. His first album, "Hato Rey" (2015), was a tribute to his San Juan neighborhood. He planned to release an album in 2016 called "Díaz Buenos, Diaz Malos," which never materialized. Shortly after, he released his second album that same year: "San Juan Grand Prix."
